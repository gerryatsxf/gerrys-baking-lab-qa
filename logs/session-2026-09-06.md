## Ingredients

| Ingredient                              | Quantity           |
|-----------------------------------------|--------------------|
| Unsalted butter (to be browned)         | 1 cup              |
| Bread flour (12.7% protein)             | 2¼ cups            |
| Dark brown sugar                        | 1 cup + 1 tbsp     |
| White sugar                             | 7 tbsp             |
| Large eggs                              | 2                  |
| Extra egg yolk                          | 1                  |
| Pure vanilla extract                    | 2 tsp              |
| Baking soda                             | 1 tsp              |
| Fine sea salt (in dough)                | 1 tsp              |
| Flaky sea salt (for topping)            | ½ tsp              |
| Dark chocolate chips (60% cacao)        | 2 cups             |

> **Intervention:** Dark brown to white sugar ratio set to approximately 70/30 by weight (total sugar: 1½ cups).
> **Control batch:** 2026-05-28 baseline (1 cup regular brown sugar + ½ cup white sugar, with brown butter + bread flour + 48h cold rest).
>
> Note: All other parameters match the 2026-05-28 optimized baseline exactly — brown butter, bread flour, 48-hour cold rest, flaky salt topping.

## Procedure

1. **Brown butter:** Cooked 1 cup unsalted butter in a light-colored saucepan over medium heat, stirring constantly, until milk solids turned golden-brown and a nutty aroma emerged (~7 min). Removed from heat immediately. Poured into mixing bowl and cooled to room temperature (~30 min).
2. Whisked browned butter with dark brown sugar (1 cup + 1 tbsp) and white sugar (7 tbsp) until fully combined.
3. Added 2 whole eggs, 1 extra yolk, and vanilla extract. Whisked vigorously for 90 seconds until mixture was pale and slightly ribbony.
4. Folded in bread flour, baking soda, and fine sea salt until just combined. Dough stiffness comparable to 2026-05-28 baseline.
5. Folded in dark chocolate chips.
6. **Cold rest:** Covered and refrigerated for exactly 48 hours at 4°C.
7. Removed dough from refrigerator. Rested at room temperature for 20 minutes.
8. Preheated oven to 375°F / 190°C. Lined two baking sheets with parchment paper.
9. Portioned into 2-tablespoon rounds spaced 5 cm apart. Topped each with a pinch of flaky sea salt.
10. Baked for 11 minutes. Edges set and golden; centers appeared underdone (correct).
11. Cooled on baking sheet 5 minutes, then wire rack 20 minutes before evaluation.
12. Baked control batch (2026-05-28 baseline) in parallel. Cookies from both batches also evaluated after 24 hours to assess shelf stability.

## Observed Results

- **Experimental (70/30 dark brown/white):** Final spread ~4.0 cm — matching the 2026-05-28 baseline. Color: deep amber, slightly darker than baseline but less so than the 100% dark brown batch. Aroma during baking: distinct molasses and caramel notes, noticeably richer than baseline without the sharp bitterness present in the 2026-06-05 batch. Flavor: complex caramel-toffee up front, hazelnut from browned butter, clean finish with no excessive bitterness. Edge: crisp and defined, comparable to baseline. Interior: chewy and moist.
- **Control (2026-05-28 baseline):** Final spread ~4.0 cm. Standard caramel flavor. Slightly lighter color. Familiar texture — crisp edge, chewy center.
- **24-hour evaluation:** Experimental cookies retained a crisp edge with only slight softening — a marked improvement over the 100% dark brown batch from 2026-06-05, which had fully lost edge crispness overnight. Shelf stability rated on par with the baseline.
- 4 out of 5 evaluators preferred experimental for flavor depth; 1 rated both equally. No evaluator preferred the control.

## Conclusions

Hypothesis **confirmed**. The 70/30 dark brown/white sugar split successfully resolves the trade-off identified in session 2026-06-05:

1. **Flavor:** The increased proportion of dark brown sugar (higher molasses content) delivered a meaningfully deeper caramel and toffee profile compared to the baseline — without the bitter finish that emerged with 100% dark brown sugar.
2. **Shelf stability recovered:** Reducing dark brown sugar from 100% to 70% brought overnight moisture retention back in line with the baseline. The excessive softening seen in session 2026-06-05 did not recur.
3. **Spread and structure unaffected:** Bread flour's gluten network continued to control spread effectively regardless of the sugar type change, confirming that the structural benefits of the 2026-05-28 baseline are robust to sugar composition adjustments.
4. **70/30 ratio is a stable optimum:** Further increasing the dark brown sugar proportion risks reintroducing the shelf stability problem; further decreasing it risks losing the flavor benefit. The 70/30 point appears to be the practical sweet spot.

**The 2026-05-28 baseline is updated:** The optimized protocol now uses a 70/30 dark brown/white sugar ratio in place of the original equal-brown/white split.

**Open questions for future sessions:**
- Salt type in the dough: fine sea salt vs. kosher salt vs. a small measure of flaky salt mixed directly into the dough (carry-over from session 2026-05-28)
- Chocolate format: standard chips vs. chopped dark chocolate bar (73% cacao) — bar chocolate creates irregular pockets that may distribute flavor differently
- Fat blend: partial substitution of browned butter with a small amount of coconut oil to test effect on chew and aroma
